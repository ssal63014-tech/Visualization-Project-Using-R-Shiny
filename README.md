# Credit Realization Trends of MSMEs by Enterprises Scale and Industry, with Insight into Financial Inclusion of Indonesia

## Overview
This narrative visualization presents an overview of credit utilization trends among Micro, Small, and Medium Enterprises (MSMEs) in Indonesia, focusing on differences across enterprise size and industry sectors. It highlights how credit is distributed, identifying industries that receive strong financial support and those that remain underserved.

The analysis also examines the role of financial inclusion in improving access to credit, particularly in marginalized regions across Indonesia’s major islands. By exploring these patterns, the visualization demonstrates how inclusive financial initiatives support MSME growth, enable business expansion, and contribute to national economic development, while also identifying gaps that require further attention.

## Key Findings
- Medium enterprises show increasing financial stress with rising NPL after 2018, while micro enterprises experience a high-risk boom-and-bust cycle, and small enterprises maintain relative stability but face worsening loan repayment issues after 2021.
- Wholesale and retail dominate credit allocation, while sectors like agriculture and manufacturing show strong growth, but utilities (electricity, gas, water) lag behind with declining credit realization.
- Financial inclusion is highly uneven, with Java leading due to population and access advantages, while regions like Maluku and Papua remain underserved despite overall post-pandemic recovery.

## Data Usage
- Indicators of Micro, Small, and Medium Enterprises (MSMEs) 2020-2024, which have been reported by the Bank Indonesia through Statistics of Indonesia’s Financial System. This data is tabular data: ~33 sheets and 161.700 cells with data. It has both categorical and numerical attributes relating to each indicator.

(https://www.bi.go.id/id/statistik/ekonomi-keuangan/sski/Pages/SSKI_Juli_2024.aspx)
- Outstanding of Micro, Small, and Medium Enterprises Credits of Commercial Banks (MSMEs), Gross Domestic Product (GDP) Deflator, and Inflation of Selected Economies 2020-2024. It has been reported by the Bank Indonesia in Indonesian Economic and Financial Statistics. This data is Tabular data: ~2 sheets and 6168 cells with data, ~2 sheets and 3524 cells with data, and 1 sheet with 1929 data cells. Each property in those datasets has both numerical and categorical attributes.

(https://www.bi.go.id/en/statistik/ekonomi-keuangan/seki/Default.aspx#headingOne)
